Yet, in the slow, painful crawl back to normalcy, he found a resilience not in some grand revelation but in the small victories of getting out of bed each day, of learning to laugh again.


And Maya, well, Maya faced a tragedy that turned her world grayscale. There were no poetic metaphors in her grief, just raw, gut-wrenching pain. She didn't dance through the darkness; she crawled, dragging her shattered pieces along.
